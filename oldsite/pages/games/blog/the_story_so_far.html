The story so far

Adventure gaming is the ultimate creative experience there is.

That said, some people have problems creating adventure games. Like me. Though there is enough material cramping up my drawers, enough ideas filling up my mind, I simply cannot go through the whole process to be able to finally put the pen, the mouse, the keyboard and everything down and say: “Eureka”.

A few weeks ago I had a simple, yet innovative idea. I'm going to free exactly one hour a week of my time to do something for my game. I don't know, if that's the final answer to my creative problem, but it's a good start after all.

So this blog I'm using to document my progress every week. Perhaps it's something, other developers can use to point them in their own direction while drawing, developing, writing, composing, whatever.

Okay. Ready? Go.

As said, a few weeks ago I started to develop my game every once in a week, exactly one hour long. I light up a candle, tune in into my favourite type of music, relax and put my fingers on the keys. I even turn out phones and other stuff, that could hinder my work.

The first thing, I looked into was creating the main character. Although I should add, that I had my little design document already nearly done. (You can have a little look at it here)

So, back to the main character. I had an idea about the overall graphic design, about a serious game with cartoon characters. I always liked cartoons, that have a mean and serious background and a dense atmosphere, but were drawn in a overall cartoonic style. So I drew my main character that way. Let's meet “Charlie”:

After I had the first sketch done, I used a technique, I've saw somewhere on the net, namely drawing him from the front and the side. Here are the scans:

Now the ugly part: Animating. I hate animating, especially when it's about walkcycles. So I needed help with that. I first tried a neat little program, an AGS community member developed. It's ssh's Walkcycle generator.

Sadly, ssh only made it to animate low-res graphics and I wanted Charlie to walk in hi-res. (Well, hi-res… I thought about a 640×480-resolution)

So. What to do? After that I tried my thing in 3d. I've seen a web tutorial on how to model characters in a 3d render environment and used this skills (sorry, I cannot describe them here in detail) to build up Charlie as a 3d model. From there it would have been easy to animate him using Inverse Kinematics. I used Blender for that.

But after building the raw model, I lost the will to carry on in 3d. I realized, that I couldn't render Charlie so that he would look like a 2d cartoon character and not like a badly rendered 3d-to-cartoon-style graphic. So I quit blender.

After that I thought: Hey, can't I use a 2D program, that knows bones and can rotate and scale images in a way the walkcycle generator did?

I nearly wasted Google for that. After a loooong search I finally found, that CreaToon was released as freeware, because the developer discontinued development.

After some experiments with CreaToon I finally got my first walkcycle for Charlie… whiiiich needs a lot of improvement and arms and hands, I know.

That's where I am at this point. Where this goes? Well, I hope to a game in the end. We'll see…

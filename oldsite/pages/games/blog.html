Learning, Learning, Learning...

This week I didn't take my hour out for my game. On one hand I had no time this tuesday as something private was taking my time that day but on the other hand I was deeply disappointed with Charlie's face and my tries to remodel it last week.

But I've surfed the web and the wonderful http://www.blenderartists.org-Forum to update my modeling techniques and found some nice tips.

One nice way to create a head was to just use a simple cube, subdivide it three times (thus giving it more vertices) and cut out the faces where the eye holes and the mouth should be. After that, add a subsurf modifier (that way, the sharp edges are more bezier-type, so the box looks a lot more round) and deform the face using blender's lattice-objects to get a nice face.

I've played around with it for some time and can present you my very first face using that method:

Err.. Well, I know it's far from being cool and all, but it's a start. I also played around with some nice eye tutorial, that basically consists of three balls, two cut in half and rotated to get the eyelids. Very simple, really. Like I said, if I can get myself to model a nice game character, I might make a tutorial out of it.

So I'll try to remodel Charlie's head next time using the methods I found. Perhaps I'll find some other methods, that make my modelling life easier.

Stay tuned.
